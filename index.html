Problem:

Blockchain hackathons should lead by example in the cryptography and privacy-minded community.


Registration currently requires giving personal identifying information (PII) to a trusted third party (TTP).


“Trusted third parties are security holes.” – Nick Szabo

FUBAR: Universal Blockchain Anonymous Registration

FUBAR is:

  • Trustless
  • Secure
  • Anonymous
  • Autonomous
  • Self-Sovereign
  • Libre
  • Open Source
  • Decentralized

Features

  • Configurable Registration Form (add the fields you need)
  • Utilizes Metamask to issue a ERC721 token used as the ticket
  • Check in with QR code, burns the ticket
  • Cancelling registration burns the ticket, increasing the event capacity

Future

  • Speaker/presentation acceptance via public vote
    • Weighted vote tokens (like Bufficoin)
  • Automated IRC/Riot/Matrix invites
  • Email verification/updates
  • Role-based permissions
    • Admin, Organizer, Volunteer, etc.

Demo

Technicality:

  • Creating an ERC721 contract, getting web3.js to invoke contract functions.

Creativity:

  • We innovated on the shoulders of Colorado Coin and Bufficoin, combining the concepts as components of a hackathon platform.

Usefulness:

  • Immediately useful as an event management platform for conferences, summits, hackathons, etc.

Design:

  • Responsive progressive web app utilizing Material Design
  • Fooby mascot image

Makes the World a Better Place:

  • Decentralizes and anonymizes personal information at blockchain events
  • Educates users about the importance of self-sovereign identity
